Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Avul Parik Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam born on 15 october 1931 - 27 july 2015. He was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. Borned and raised in Muslim famiy Rameswaraam, Tamil Nadu, he studied Physics & aerospace engineering He spent next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Difference Research and Development Organisation(DRDO) and Indian Space Research (ISRO) and was initimately involved in India's civilian space programme and millitary missile development efforts. He thus came to be known as the Missile Man of the India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vechile technology. He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and pilictical role in India's Pokhran-|| nuclear test in 1998, the first sice the original nuclear test by India in 1974.

Achievements of Abdul Kalam

Abdul Kalam was a person of golden heart who has received ample awardand achieved many things during his life journey. In 1981 Abdul Kalam received the prestigious Padma Bhushan award. The renowned personality, because of his tremendous effort towards the nation, received the Bharat Ratna in 1997. In the same year, he was awarded for National Integration. The Government of India awarded Kalam with the Veer Savarkar award in 1998. Because of his contribution to arts, science, and technology, he received the SASTRA Ramanujan prize in 2000.
